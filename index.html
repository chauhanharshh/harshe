Aerin & Rahul
March 1, 2025

Worlds collide in a Symphony of Love.
Two worlds came together, blending colours in a very only love run.

Aerin, with the quiet grace of her Korean heritage and Rahul, carrying the vibrant warmth of Indian traditions, found each other across continents and languages — their story beginning by chance, yet unfolding like destiny. Two lives from opposite ends of the world moving instinctively closer, as if the universe had been gently aligning their paths all along.

Fire and silk.
Places and portraits.
Language is nothing against heartbeats.
Vows sung, knees exchanged.
Families folded gather in prayer, but as one.

As the sun dipped behind Lake Picola, the light turned everything gold — every glance softer, every smile deeper. In those quiet in-between moments you could feel it: this wasn't fusion for spectacle. It was belonging.

Two histories. Two languages. Two traditions learning each other's rhythms.
And in the end nothing felt divided — only expanded.

Because love did what it does best: it collided with cultures and created something entirely new, something beautiful, something entirely their own.

Mehar & Juan
February 28, 2025

A Garden of Two Worlds.
This one hit me with a full heart.

Mehar and Juan aren't just a couple I photographed, they became friends. The kind that laugh with you between shots, trust you with their silences, and remind you why you do what you do in the first place. Two weeks spent on paper, but watching them together, you'd never know they weren't always side by side. Their love is calm, steady, deeply rooted. Like the trees that surrounded us, like the land we stood on. Every moment felt ordained. Organic. Like it had always been trying to happen.

From the way she looked at him through the dance, to the quiet walk they took across the field before the ceremony — it didn't feel like I was documenting a wedding. It felt like I was witnessing a story I already believed in.

So here it is.
For Mehar & Juan.
For love that crosses borders and becomes something much bigger — Home.
